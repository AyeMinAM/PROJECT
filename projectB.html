Koo Golf
I-Design inside the Browser.

Koo Golf is a web and mobile platform for golf enthusiasts worldwide. Golf players can book tee time online and purchase customized golf-related tour packages and activities. Because of the time constraint, I designed the app inside the chrome browser straight from the sketches and wireframes skipping the step of creating the design mockups.

Wireframes & Sketches

The content on the home page is displayed based on the user’s location. If a user turns on the GPS, the user will see golf courses and packages based on his location. Otherwise, he will see golf courses and popular golf packages generated by the system. The universal search bar is on every header of the pages where users can search for golf courses, golf packages, and the destination cities where they can find the golf courses and packages.

From the home page, users might land on one of the three pages: packages, cities, and destinations. Users can further browse in these for more golf courses and packages. From these pages, the users can navigate to the tour package details and course page. Please see the following wireframes for the concepts.

Final Design in Browser

Koo Golf was created targeting mainly mobile users (80%). As a result, my responsibility as a UI/UX designer, was to create a responsive design mainly catered for mobile users.

Typography

One of the challenges I faced while working on this project was picking the right Chinese font since I am not familiar with the Chinese language. After doing research on Chinese fonts and having discussions with the product owner, I learned that there are two versions: Simplified Chinese, which is used in mainland China since 1949, and Traditional Chinese, which a much more complex version which is still used in Taiwan. Similar to English fonts being classified as San-serif and Serif fonts, there are two classifications for Chinese fonts: Songti & Heiti (a more modern font). Since Koo Golf targets mostly mainland China golfers, I chose Microsoft YaHei which also matches the minimalist design of the product.
